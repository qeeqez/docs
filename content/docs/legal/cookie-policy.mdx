# Cookie Policy



**Last Updated: September 2025**



## What Are Cookies



Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit our website or use our services. They improve your experience by saving preferences, maintaining login sessions, and providing insights into platform usage.



## How We Use Cookies



RIXL uses cookies and similar technologies for several purposes:



### Essential Cookies

These cookies are required for our website and services to function properly. They enable core features such as:

- User authentication and login sessions

- Security features and fraud prevention

- Basic website functionality and navigation

- Load balancing and performance optimization



Essential cookies are necessary for our services and cannot be disabled.



### Performance and Analytics Cookies

These cookies collect anonymous data to help us understand how visitors interact with our website and services, including:

- Pages visited and time spent on each page

- User interactions and feature usage

- Error messages and technical issues

- Traffic sources and user journeys



We use this data to enhance our services and your experience.



### Functional Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization, such as remembering your preferences and settings..

- Customizing your dashboard and interface

- Storing your language and region preferences

- Maintaining your media processing preferences



### Marketing and Advertising Cookies

With your consent, we use these cookies to:

- Measure the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns

- Provide personalized content recommendations

- Track conversions and user engagement



## Types of Cookies We Use



### Session Cookies

Session cookies are deleted when you close your browser and help maintain your session during use.



### Persistent Cookies

Persistent cookies remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them. They remember your preferences across visits.



### First-Party Cookies

These cookies are set by RIXL to provide core services and functionality.



### Third-Party Cookies

Third-party cookies are set by our partners and service providers, such as analytics providers (for example, Google Analytics).

- Payment processors

- Customer support tools

- Content delivery networks



## Managing Your Cookie Preferences



### Browser Settings

You can manage cookies using your browser settings. For Chrome: Settings > Privacy and Security > Cookies and other site data.

**Firefox**: Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data

**Safari**: Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data

**Edge**: Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Cookies and site data



### Cookie Consent

When you first visit our website, a cookie consent banner allows you to:

- Accept all cookies

- Reject non-essential cookies

- Customize your cookie preferences

- Learn more about our cookie practices



You can update your preferences at any time by clicking the "Cookie Preferences" link in the website footer.



Opt-Out Options

To opt out of specific cookies, use the following options:

- **Google Analytics**: [Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on](https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout)

- **Advertising Cookies**: [Your Online Choices](http://www.youronlinechoices.com/) or [Network Advertising Initiative](http://www.networkadvertising.org/choices/)



## Impact of Disabling Cookies



Disabling certain cookies may limit the functionality of websites and services. For example, you may need to log in repeatedly.

- Your preferences and settings may not be saved

- Some features may not work as expected

- We may not be able to provide personalized experiences



Essential cookies are necessary for the basic functionality of the website and cannot be disabled.



## Other Tracking Technologies



We also use additional tracking technologies alongside cookies:



### Web Beacons

These are small, transparent images that help us understand user behavior and email engagement.



### Local Storage

Local storage maintains your preferences and improves performance.



### SDKs and APIs

Our software development kits collect usage data to enhance functionality and performance.



### Log Files

Log files record technical details about your interactions with our services.



## Data Protection and Privacy



### Data Security

We implement security measures to protect cookie data and ensure it is used only for its intended purposes.



### Data Retention

Cookies are retained for different periods depending on their purpose:

- Session cookies: Deleted when you close your browser

- Functional cookies: Typically 1-2 years

- Analytics cookies: Usually 2 years

- Marketing cookies: Varies by provider, typically 1-2 years



### International Transfers

Some cookie data may be processed by service providers in other countries. We ensure safeguards are in place for these transfers.



## Children's Privacy



Our services are not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect cookie data from individuals under the age of 18. If you believe a child has provided us with cookie data, please contact us.

Handle incoming invitations to join organizations and projects, and track your invitation history.

## Pending Invitations

View and respond to invitations from organizations and project teams.

### Invitation Overview

When you have pending invitations, you'll see:
- **Organization/Project Name**: Where you're being invited
- **Invited By**: Who sent the invitation
- **Proposed Role**: Your intended role and permissions
- **Invitation Date**: When the invitation was sent
- **Expiration**: When the invitation expires (if applicable)

### Managing Invitations

<Steps>
    <Step>
        **Review Invitation Details:**
        - Click on any invitation to see full details
        - Review the role and permissions being offered
        - Check the organization's profile and current members
    </Step>

    <Step>
        **Accept or Decline:**
        - Click **Accept** to join the organization/project
        - Click **Decline** to politely refuse
        - Add an optional message when declining
    </Step>

    <Step>
        **Immediate Access:**
        - Accepted invitations grant immediate access
        - You'll see the new organization in your membership list
        - Access granted projects and team resources right away
    </Step>
</Steps>

### When You Have No Invitations

If you have no pending invitations, you'll see:
- **"No invites found"** message
- **Refresh** button to check for new invitations
- Option to request invitations from known organizations

<Callout type="info">
Click **Refresh** periodically to check for new invitations, as they may arrive between page visits.
</Callout>

## Invitation Types

### Organization Invitations

Join entire organizations with access to multiple projects:
- **Team Collaboration**: Work with organization members
- **Project Access**: Gain access to current and future projects
- **Resource Sharing**: Use organization-wide resources and settings

### Project-Specific Invitations

Join individual projects without full organization membership:
- **Limited Scope**: Access only to the specific project
- **Focused Collaboration**: Work with project team members
- **Temporary Access**: Often for contractors or external collaborators

### Role-Based Invitations

Invitations include specific role assignments:
- **Member**: Standard access for content creation and collaboration
- **Admin**: Enhanced permissions for project management
- **Custom Roles**: Organization-specific roles with tailored permissions

## Invitation Security

### Verifying Legitimate Invitations

Before accepting invitations:

<Steps>
    <Step>
        **Verify the Sender:**
        - Check if you recognize the person who sent the invitation
        - Look up the organization to confirm legitimacy
        - Contact the sender directly if unsure
    </Step>

    <Step>
        **Review Permissions Carefully:**
        - Understand what access you're granting
        - Check if the role matches your expectations
        - Consider potential security implications
    </Step>

    <Step>
        **Organization Reputation:**
        - Research the organization if it's unfamiliar
        - Check their public projects and team members
        - Verify their business or contact information
    </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warn" title="Security Notice">
Only accept invitations from trusted sources. Malicious invitations could grant unwanted access to your content or expose your profile to unauthorized users.
</Callout>

## Troubleshooting Invitations

### Common Issues

**Invitation not received:**
1. Check your spam/junk email folders
2. Verify your email address in account settings
3. Ask the sender to resend the invitation

**Can't accept invitation:**
1. Check if the invitation has expired
2. Verify you're logged into the correct account
3. Ensure you meet any organization requirements

**Wrong role assigned:**
1. Contact the organization administrator
2. Request role modification after joining
3. Clarify expectations before accepting

**Invitation expired:**
1. Contact the sender to request a new invitation
2. Check if the organization still needs team members
3. Apply through alternative channels if available
